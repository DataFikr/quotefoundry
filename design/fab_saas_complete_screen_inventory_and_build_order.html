17 of 20 MVP screens designed. Only 3 small ones remain — add/edit customer form, trial paywall, and manage plan — and two of those are mostly Stripe's hosted UI. The design phase is essentially complete; what's left is the build.
1
Foundation & auth
Nothing works until this exists
~1 week
Set up Supabase: database, auth provider, Row-Level Security policies keyed to shop_id
Build login, sign-up (creates shop tenant), password reset
Define the core data model: shops, users, quotes, customers, rates
Milestone: a user can sign up, log in, and their data is isolated from every other shop.
2
The quote engine + editor
The differentiating core — build it second, while energy is high
~1.5 weeks
Onboarding: pick industry, enter rates (pre-filled), save to rate library
Quote editor with the live cost build-up, snapshotting rates per quote
Save a quote, reopen it (quote detail view)
Milestone: a shop can build and save an accurate quote in minutes — the demo moment works.
3
Output: PDF + email send
Turn a quote into something that wins work
~1 week
Branded PDF generation (hide margin, show scope + total)
Email send via transactional provider — the SPF/DKIM/DMARC work lives here
Timestamp + open tracking logged to the quote
Milestone: end-to-end — build a quote, send it, and it lands in the customer's inbox.
4
The spine: pipeline, customers, rates
The daily-use connective tissue
~1.5 weeks
Pipeline home — list, search, filters, status
Customer list + inline add/edit form; wire into the editor
Clone quote, edit-rates settings, mark won/lost
Milestone: the full daily loop is usable — log in, find, clone, quote, send, track.
5
Billing — last
Only needed the day before your first paid conversion
~1 week
Stripe Checkout + Billing, trial logic, paywall at trial end
Manage-plan screen (mostly Stripe's hosted portal)
Hand-invoice your first design partners while this is built in parallel
Milestone: trials convert to paid automatically. You have a business, not just a product.
Roughly 6 weeks of focused solo build to a sellable MVP — though land your first design-partner shops during phase 3–4, not after phase 5. Get the product in front of shops the moment the quote-and-send loop works; billing can trail.
